no laughing gas

The rise in greenhouse gas emissions has consequences for climate change and environmental health. Among these gases, is nitrous oxide (Nâ‚‚O). stands out as a potent contributor, with measured trapping power to atmospheric heat 300 times that of carbon dioxide and a half-life exceeding 100 years. The gas originates largely from agriculture, which contributes nearly 60% of the emissions, mainly through the bacterial oxidation of nitrogen fertilizers in the soil, a process named nitrification and denitrification. The resulting emissions, coupled with nitrogen fertilizer leaching in the form of nitrites, not only diminish plant nitrogen uptake but also necessitate increased fertilizer use, affecting soil health and mineral concentrations, and more importantly, endangered climate conditions.
